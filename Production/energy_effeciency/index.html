Unlocking Energy Efficiency with Windows in Ontario

In Ontario, where the seasons can swing from scorching summers to icy winters, the right windows play a pivotal role in maintaining a comfortable indoor environment. More than just a view to the outside world, windows are integral to a building's energy dynamics, influencing both the warmth and coolness inside. By opting for energy-efficient windows, you're not just ensuring a cozy living space but also making strides in reducing energy bills and environmental impact.

Energy Star Ratings

The Energy Star label, a trusted emblem backed by governmental standards, signifies top-tier energy efficiency. Windows that sport this label have met stringent criteria, ensuring they're up to the task of handling Ontario's diverse climate. Beyond the badge, there are key metrics that define a window's energy prowess:

U-FACTOR

Think of the U-factor as the guardian against the cold. It gauges a window's insulation caliber, determining how well it can keep the warmth in during those chilly months. The lower the U-factor, the better its insulating properties.

SOLAR HEAT GAIN COEFFICIENT (SHGC)

As the sun blazes, the SHGC steps in, measuring a window's ability to fend off the sun's heat. A window with a lower SHGC is like having a built-in sunshade, keeping interiors cool when temperatures soar.

VISUAL TRANSMITTANCE

Beyond temperature, there's light. VT assesses how much natural sunlight a window lets in. A higher VT means brighter, sunlit spaces, reducing the need for artificial lights and setting a vibrant indoor mood.
